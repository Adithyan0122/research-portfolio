# Technical Analysis: v0.3 — Full App (Neural)

This report evaluates the transition from sparse (TF-IDF) to dense (Neural) retrieval and the performance of persistence mechanisms.

## 1. Experimental Setup
- **Model**: Mistral (Ollama)
- **Embedding**: `all-MiniLM-L6-v2` (Dense/Neural)
- **Persistence**: NumPy Binary (`.npy`) + JSON Metadata
- **Top-K**: 3

---

## 2. Neural Benchmark Results

| Strategy | Hit Rate | MRR | Faithfulness | Context Precision |
| :--- | :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: |
| **Fixed-size** | 20% | 0.20 | 100% | 26% |
| **Paragraph** | **100%** | **1.00** | **100%** | 46% |
| **Semantic** | 40% | 0.40 | 80% | 33% |

---

## 3. The "Neural Bridge" Discovery

### The 40% Jump
In v0.2 (TF-IDF), the **Semantic** strategy achieved **0% Hit Rate**. In v0.3 (Neural), it jumped to **40%**.
- **Transformation**: Neural embeddings allow the retriever to match the *meaning* of a sentence even if the literal words (like "TF-IDF" vs "hand-rolled term frequency") differ.
- **Limitation**: Even with neural bridge, very small chunks (single sentences) often lack enough local context to answer "Why" or "How" questions independently.

### The Paragraph Dominance
Paragraph-based splitting remains the "Golden Baseline" for documents with clear structural breaks. 
- **Neural Synergy**: Combined with neural retrieval, it achieved a perfect **1.00 Faithfulness**, as the embeddings selected the exact correct paragraph, and the large context provided the LLM with all necessary grounded facts.

---

## 4. Feature Validation: Persistence
- **Performance**: Indexing the 860-word corpus took ~10.8s (mostly model loading). 
- **Reloading**: Loading the saved index via `load_index()` took **0.002s**. 
- **Scaling**: v0.3 proves that persistence is the requirement for "Full App" scaling, decoupling ingestion time from query time.

---

## 5. Failure Analysis (The Next Frontier)
While v0.3 solves retrieval bottlenecks, it introduces **Structural Noise** from PDFs. Table parsing and multi-column layouts in PDFs remain "unsolved" in this raw implementation, setting the stage for v0.4 (Hybrid Search).
