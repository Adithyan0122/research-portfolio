# Technical Analysis: v0.2 — Chunking Comparator

This report benchmarks four distinct document splitting strategies against the core RAG metrics.

## 1. Experimental Setup
- **Model**: Mistral (Ollama)
- **Embedding**: TF-IDF (Sparse)
- **Corpus**: RAG/NLP Demo (860 words)
- **Top-K**: 3

---

## 2. Benchmark Results

| Strategy | Hit Rate | MRR | Faithfulness | Context Precision |
| :--- | :---: | :---: | :---: | :---: |
| **Fixed-size (80/15)** | 20% | 0.20 | 100% | 20% |
| **Sentence-based** | 0% | 0.00 | 100% | 53% |
| **Paragraph-based** | **100%** | **1.00** | 80% | 40% |
| **Semantic (TF-IDF)** | 0% | 0.00 | 100% | 53% |

---

## 3. Critical Observations

### Why did Paragraph win?
The Paragraph strategy achieved a perfect **100% Hit Rate** and **1.00 MRR**.
- **Reasoning**: In our demo corpus, each paragraph is a self-contained topic (e.g., "What is RAG", "How TF-IDF works"). By splitting on double newlines, we preserved the entire context required for each evaluation question.
- **Trade-off**: Lower **Faithfulness (80%)**. Because paragraphs are larger (~43 words avg), the LLM received more information than it "needed," leading to slightly noisier (though accurate) generations.

### The Failure of Small Chunks (Sentence/Semantic)
Both Sentence and Semantic strategies (using current thresholds) resulted in small chunks (avg 13 words).
- **The TF-IDF Trap**: Sparse vectorizers like TF-IDF perform poorly on short text because there is almost no overlap between query terms and chunk terms if the chunk is only one sentence.
- **Neural Bridge**: v0.2 demonstrates the necessity of neural embeddings (coming in future versions) if one wishes to use fine-grained chunking.

## 4. Visual Comparison
